🏘️ Our story

Built for one girl.
Grown for many.

Homework Town started with a single problem: every afternoon, getting one little girl to sit down and do her homework felt impossible. Tanit Ansara — her au pair and a BCom Information Systems student at UCT — had tried everything. Sticker charts, timers, bribes. Nothing stuck.

So she built something. A small game where finishing homework earned coins, and coins bought things for a virtual town. That evening, the little girl asked if she could do more homework to unlock the shop.

Tanit turned it into Homework Town — because she didn't want other parents and au pairs to dread the 3pm homework battle, and she didn't want kids to either. We recently launched in beta and the response has already blown us away.

"I'm an au pair and a student — I'm not a developer by trade. I just knew this little girl, knew what made her tick, and built something around that. When she stopped fighting homework, I knew other kids could too."

👩‍💻
Tanit Ansara
Founder · Au pair · BCom IS student, UCT

📅 How we got here

Our journey

🌱
The problem
One little girl, one big battle
Tanit Ansara, au pair and BCom Information Systems student at UCT, watched the same scene play out every afternoon — homework was a fight. She tried everything. None of it worked.
💡
The idea
What if homework unlocked something fun?
Drawing on her IS studies, Tanit built a simple prototype: a virtual town where completing real homework tasks earned virtual coins. The little girl she looks after tried it that evening.
✨
The moment
She asked to do more homework
That first night, the little girl finished her work and asked if she could do more so she could unlock the shop. Homework went from a battle to something she looked forward to.
🚀
Now
Beta is live — open to everyone
Tanit turned her solution into Homework Town and launched in beta. We want every parent, au pair, and tutor who dreads homework time to have access to the same thing that worked for one little girl in Cape Town.
